Introduction

Contemporary standards for the construction of measuring instruments include integrated systems with open networks, operating in the areas of the Internet or cloud. The technical solutions of these devices make their software vulnerable to the adverse impact of the environment in which IT functions in particular. According to estimates, measuring instruments participate in 4 to 6 percent of the value of all transactions in the European Union. Among other things, these instruments are subject to legal control before they appear on the EU market. Therefore, the issue of conformity of risk assessment of the use of measuring instruments within the EU common market is of great importance, which is connected with the need to base its assessment methods on the applicable international standards, such as ISO /IEC 27005 i 15408.

The method proposed here contains elements derived from the international software security standard referring to the ISO / IEC 15408 risk assessment, also known as the Common Criteria. The premise for the creation of works including methods of risk assessment in measuring instruments is the UE Directive (MID) 2014/32 / EU.
