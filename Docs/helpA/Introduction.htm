Introduction

 

Measuring instruments constructed today can be integrated with open networks, operating in areas such as the Internet or clouds. As a result software of measuring instruments are in danger of an attack by the environment the in which they operate. It is estimated that measuring instruments participate in 4 to 6 percent of the value of all transactions in the European Union. Therefore, they are subject to legal control before being placed on the EU market. The risk of using measuring instruments within the EU common market is assessed in accordance with the applicable international standards:
ISO / IEC 27005 and 15408.

The Rami program contains software security standards in accordance with ISO / IEC 15408, also known as Common Criteria. The premise for the creation of works including. The methods of hazard assessment in measuring instruments are specified in the UE Directive (MID) 2014/32 / EU.
